Satyajit Ray

2 May 1921 - 23 April 1992

"The only solutions that are ever worth anything are the solutions that people find themselves"
About Ray...

Born into a family of reputed artists, Satyajit Ray (1921—1992) was a motion-film director, writer, illustrator and composer. Often touted as the figure that put Indian cinema on the world map, Ray earned eleven international prizes for his masterpiece “Pather Panchali”, including an honorary Oscar in 1992 for his contribution to cinema. He directed 36 independent films which included short films and documentaries. Besides films, Ray also wrote novels and short fiction predominantly for children and young adults. Among others, characters like Feluda, the skilful sleuth and Professor Shonku, the sharp-witted scientist are popular till date. The Government of India awarded him the Bharat Ratna – the highest civilian award in the country – shortly before his death.
Martin Scorsese described the work of Satyajit Ray as “treasures of cinema” that should be watched by “everyone with an interest in film”. The Japanese master Akira Kurosawa went further: “Not to have seen the cinema of Ray means existing in the world without seeing the sun or the moon.”

Incredible versatility of Ray...

Film Director


Satyajit Ray's contribution to the film industry is immense. He directed a total of 36 films, including feature films, documentaries, and short films. His most famous work is the Apu Trilogy, which consists of three films - Pather Panchali, Aparajito, and Apur Sansar.


Author


Apart from filmmaking, Ray was also a writer and wrote several novels and short stories. His most famous work is the Feluda series of detective novels, which features the character Feluda, a detective who solves crimes with his intelligence and wit.


Music Composer


Ray was also a talented music composer and arranger, and he often composed the music for his films. He was well-versed in Indian classical music and was influenced by Western classical music and jazz.


Graphic Designer


Satyajit Ray was also an accomplished designer, and his iconic film posters, book covers, and other graphic designs are still revered for their simplicity and elegance. His designs were often characterized by a minimalist approach and the use of bold, primary colors.
